## Summary

Enhanced the `/fix-it` play with anti-reward-hacking mechanisms and a leaner task-list discipline. Added 6 constraints (C14–C19) covering TDD invariant, validator-not-implementer (new quality-auditor agent), independent signal, contract-level test-file isolation, play-owned task list, and retry cap=2 with warn-and-continue re-plan. Removed C5 (risk assessment). Rebaked SKILL.md from new intent via `/create-play --build fix-it`.

## Problem

`/fix-it` had three anti-patterns enabling reward hacking: code-builder self-certified its own fix, there was no regression-test artifact anchoring the implementer to a concrete pass condition, and no durable play-owned record of task progress existed. Risk-assessment ceremony (C5) had no downstream consumer for lean bug-fix work.

## Solution Applied

Per `approach.yaml` — 6 new constraints added to `fix-it/intent.yaml`:
- **C14 TDD invariant** — tech-designer authors failing regression-test artifact before code-builder runs
- **C15 Validator-not-implementer** — quality-auditor executes verification, never code-builder
- **C16 Independent signal** — pass/fail verdict from quality-auditor report, never builder self-report
- **C17 Contract-level isolation** — code-builder contract carries regression_test_path in read_only_files, never test content
- **C18 Play-owned task list** — status/fix-it.json extended with task_list array; play is sole writer
- **C19 Retry cap + re-plan** — cap=2 per task; warn-and-continue on re-plan growth, no halt, no escalation

Removed C5 (risks). Rebaked SKILL.md (586 lines, intent_hash `9c6df454…`, 14 SE + 4 SCE). Added `quality-auditor` as 4th domain agent with new Step 6b verify-fix between implement and ship.
